# george boole

![Portrait](images/portrait.jpg)
*George Boole — the man who turned logic into math, and accidentally made computers possible.*

**Field:** Mathematics, Logic

- **Lifespan:** 1815–1864
- **Key contribution:** Boolean algebra — the mathematical system underlying all digital computing
- **Impact:** Every computer chip in existence runs on Boolean logic. Every. Single. One.

## Biography

George Boole was a self-taught English mathematician and philosopher who created Boolean algebra — a system of logic using just two values: TRUE and FALSE (or 1 and 0). He had no idea that his abstract mathematical framework would, a century later, become the foundation of all digital circuits and computer programming.

He also died at 49 because his wife poured cold water on him while he had pneumonia, believing the cure for something should resemble its cause. Victorian medicine was... creative.

### Early Life

- Born on November 2, 1815, in Lincoln, England
- His father was a shoemaker and amateur scientist with limited means — Boole was largely self-educated
- Taught himself Latin, Greek, French, German, and Italian by age 14 (because apparently being a math genius wasn't enough)
- Had little formal education beyond primary school but read voraciously, especially mathematics
- At 16, he became an assistant teacher to support his family. By 19, he opened his own school
- Self-studied the works of Laplace and Lagrange — heavy-duty mathematics, learned without tutors

### Career

- **1838:** Published his first mathematical paper, bringing him to the attention of the academic community
- **1844:** Won the Royal Medal from the Royal Society for his paper on differential equations — remarkable for someone without a university degree
- **1847:** Published *The Mathematical Analysis of Logic*, arguing that logic should be considered a branch of mathematics, not philosophy. This was a radical idea at the time.
- **1849:** Appointed the first Professor of Mathematics at Queen's College, Cork (now University College Cork), Ireland — despite having no degree himself
- **1854:** Published *An Investigation of the Laws of Thought*, his masterwork, which laid out Boolean algebra in full

### Later Life & Legacy

- Married Mary Everest (niece of George Everest, after whom the mountain is named) in 1855
- They had five daughters, several of whom became notable in their own right — Alicia Boole Stott made significant contributions to four-dimensional geometry
- Died on December 8, 1864, at age 49, from pneumonia
- His work was largely considered abstract and theoretical during his lifetime
- Claude Shannon rediscovered Boolean algebra in the 1930s and showed it was the perfect mathematical framework for designing digital circuits — and the rest is (computing) history

## Major Contributions

### Boolean Algebra

The mathematical system that makes digital computing possible.

**Year(s):** 1847–1854

**Context:** Logic had been a branch of philosophy since Aristotle. Boole wanted to give it mathematical rigor — to create an "algebra of logic" where reasoning could be expressed as equations.

**Technical Details:**
- Reduced logic to algebra using just two values: **1** (TRUE) and **0** (FALSE)
- Defined three fundamental operations:
  - **AND** (conjunction): A ∧ B — true only when both A and B are true
  - **OR** (disjunction): A ∨ B — true when either A or B is true
  - **NOT** (negation): ¬A — inverts the value
- Showed that complex logical statements could be expressed and simplified as algebraic equations
- Proved that logical reasoning follows mathematical laws, just like arithmetic

**Impact:** In 1937, Claude Shannon demonstrated that Boolean algebra could be used to design electrical switching circuits — the basis of all digital computers. Every logic gate in every processor, every `if` statement in every program, every database query — all Boolean algebra. Boole essentially wrote the mathematical language that computers think in.

### The Laws of Thought

Boole's magnum opus, which formalized the relationship between logic and mathematics.

**Year(s):** 1854

**Context:** Building on his 1847 work, Boole wanted to create a comprehensive mathematical framework for human reasoning itself.

**Technical Details:**
- Full title: *An Investigation of the Laws of Thought, on Which Are Founded the Mathematical Theories of Logic and Probabilities*
- Extended Boolean algebra to handle probabilities
- Introduced the concept of a "universe of discourse" — the set of all things being discussed
- Showed how to translate English logical statements into mathematical equations and solve them

**Impact:** Established the field of mathematical logic. Influenced later logicians like Augustus De Morgan, Gottlob Frege, Bertrand Russell, and ultimately the theoretical foundations of computer science.

## Publications & Works

### Seminal Papers
- **"On a General Method in Analysis"** (1844) — Won the Royal Medal; established his mathematical reputation
- **"The Mathematical Analysis of Logic"** (1847) — First work on symbolic logic; argued logic is a branch of mathematics

### Books
- **"An Investigation of the Laws of Thought"** (1854) — The foundational text of Boolean algebra and mathematical logic. A book that would wait 80+ years to find its greatest application.
- **"A Treatise on Differential Equations"** (1859) — Major contribution to differential equations
- **"A Treatise on the Calculus of Finite Differences"** (1860) — Important mathematical reference

## Influence & Legacy

### Direct Impact
- Created the mathematical framework (Boolean algebra) that underlies all digital computing
- Established mathematical logic as a legitimate field of study
- Bridged philosophy and mathematics by formalizing logical reasoning

### Indirect Impact
- Claude Shannon's 1937 master's thesis directly applied Boolean algebra to circuit design — arguably the most important master's thesis ever written
- Influenced the development of set theory, probability theory, and formal logic
- His work enabled the design of every digital computer, smartphone, and electronic device

### Modern Relevance
- Boolean logic is taught in every computer science curriculum worldwide
- Every programming language includes Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT)
- Database queries (SQL), search engines, and digital circuit design all rely on Boolean algebra
- The `bool` data type in programming is named after him
