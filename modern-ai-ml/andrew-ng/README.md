# Andrew Ng

**Field:** Machine Learning, AI Education, Deep Learning

- **Lifespan:** b. 1976 (London, England)
- **Key Contributions:** Google Brain, Coursera, deeplearning.ai, Landing AI, AI education democratization
- **Impact:** Made machine learning accessible to millions through online education; led foundational deep learning research at Google and Baidu

---

## Early Life & Education

Andrew Ng was born in London in 1976, to parents who had emigrated from Hong Kong. He grew up in Hong Kong and Singapore before heading west for what can only be described as an academically aggressive college career. At Carnegie Mellon University, he earned a BS with a *triple major* in computer science, statistics, and economics — because apparently one major is for quitters. He then picked up an MS from MIT before completing his PhD at UC Berkeley in 2002, studying under Michael Jordan. (The machine learning one, not the basketball one. This is a common source of confusion and occasional disappointment at dinner parties.)

His doctoral work focused on reinforcement learning and its application to robotics, which set the stage for a career spent at the intersection of theory, engineering, and — eventually — teaching the entire planet how neural networks work.

---

## Career & Major Contributions

### Stanford & the Course That Changed Everything

Ng joined the Stanford faculty in 2002 and quickly became known for his unusually clear and engaging teaching style. His Stanford machine learning course became legendary on campus, but the real fireworks started in 2011 when he offered it online for free. Over **100,000 students** enrolled. This wasn't just a popular course — it was the event that essentially kickstarted the MOOC (Massive Open Online Course) revolution. Higher education would never look quite the same again.

If you've ever taken an online course from a university, you can trace part of that lineage back to Ng's 2011 experiment. He's been called the "Godfather of MOOCs," or at the very least, the "Cool Uncle of MOOCs."

### Coursera (2012)

Riding the wave of that wildly successful online class, Ng co-founded **Coursera** in 2012 with fellow Stanford professor Daphne Koller. The platform grew into one of the largest online learning destinations in the world. Ng's own Machine Learning course on Coursera has been taken by **millions** of students, making it quite possibly the most popular online course in the history of computer science. If you've ever heard someone say "I learned ML from Andrew Ng," you are talking to one of a *very* large club.

### Google Brain (2011) — Teaching Computers to Find Cats

Ng founded and led **Google Brain**, Google's deep learning research project, starting in 2011. The team's most famous early result? The "cat neuron." They trained a massive unsupervised neural network on random frames from YouTube videos — no labels, no instructions, no "hey, look for cats." The network spontaneously learned to recognize cats. Nobody told it about cats. It just... found them. On its own. On the internet. Which, if you think about it, is exactly what the rest of us do too.

This result was a landmark demonstration that large-scale unsupervised learning could discover meaningful high-level features from raw data. It captured the public imagination and helped ignite the modern deep learning boom.

### Chief Scientist at Baidu (2014–2017)

In 2014, Ng moved to **Baidu** — China's largest search engine — as Chief Scientist, where he led the company's AI strategy and research. His team made significant advances in deep learning applied to speech recognition, pushing the boundaries of what was possible in voice-powered interfaces. He led a research group of over 1,300 people, because at a certain point your lab is less a "team" and more a "small army."

### deeplearning.ai (2017)

After leaving Baidu, Ng founded **deeplearning.ai**, an education platform offering specialization courses in deep learning, TensorFlow, NLP, and more. The Deep Learning Specialization on Coursera became another massive hit, bringing cutting-edge AI concepts to hundreds of thousands of new learners. If his original ML course was the gateway drug, deeplearning.ai was the full curriculum.

### Landing AI (2017) & Data-Centric AI

Ng also founded **Landing AI** in 2017, with the mission of bringing AI to manufacturing and other traditional industries that hadn't yet been touched by the deep learning revolution. Through Landing AI, he became a leading advocate for **data-centric AI** — the idea that instead of endlessly tweaking model architectures, we should focus on improving the *quality* of the data we feed into them. It's a deceptively simple insight that has reshaped how many practitioners think about real-world AI deployment.

### AI Fund

Ng launched the **AI Fund**, a venture studio focused on building AI-powered companies from the ground up. It's part incubator, part investment vehicle, and part expression of Ng's belief that AI will eventually transform virtually every industry on the planet.

## Key Technical Contributions

1. **Sparse autoencoder research** — foundational work on learning efficient representations in neural networks
2. **Large-scale unsupervised learning** — the "cat neuron" paper demonstrated that deep networks could discover high-level concepts without supervision
3. **Deep learning for speech recognition** — led breakthroughs at Baidu that advanced the state of the art in voice AI
4. **Data-centric AI** — shifted the conversation from model-centric to data-centric approaches for practical ML systems

## Philosophy & Famous Quotes

Ng is perhaps best known for his rallying cry:

> "AI is the new electricity."

He's elaborated on this with characteristic clarity:

> "Just as electricity transformed almost everything 100 years ago, today I actually have a hard time thinking of an industry that I don't think AI will transform in the next several years."

His core belief is that AI isn't just for tech companies — it's a general-purpose technology that will reshape healthcare, agriculture, manufacturing, education, and pretty much everything else. He has been one of the loudest and most credible voices arguing that AI literacy should be as universal as reading and writing.

He is also known for his incredibly patient, step-by-step teaching style. He has a gift for making complex mathematics feel approachable, and he has almost certainly taught more people machine learning than any other single human in history.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Andrew Ng occupies a unique position in the AI world: he is simultaneously a serious researcher, a tech industry leader, and arguably the most effective AI educator who has ever lived. He helped build Google's deep learning capabilities, led AI strategy for China's biggest tech company, and then turned around and made sure that anyone with an internet connection could learn how all of it works.

His influence extends far beyond any single paper or product. Millions of working ML engineers got their start with one of his courses. The MOOC revolution he helped spark has changed how universities think about access and education. And his data-centric AI movement is quietly reshaping how industry deploys machine learning in the real world.

If deep learning is the engine, Andrew Ng is the guy who wrote the driver's manual — and then handed it out for free.
